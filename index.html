01 / What We're About

Understanding the city we live in.


Every building in New York has a story — who designed it, who financed it, and why it matters. The Architecture & Real Estate Club (ARC) is where Browning students dig into those stories.

We break down real projects: How did Hudson Yards get built? Why does a one-bedroom in Tribeca cost $2M? What makes the Guggenheim a masterpiece? Each week, we tackle a new question through case studies, guest discussions, and hands-on design thinking.

No experience needed — just curiosity about the world above, below, and around you.

02 / Core Pillars

Foundations

Everything we study connects back to one question: how does a city get built?
01

Architecture & Design

Study iconic NYC buildings — from the Oculus to One57. Learn to read floor plans, understand structural systems, and debate what makes great design.

02

Real Estate & Finance

Break down how buildings get funded and sold. We analyze real deals, study market trends, and learn how zoning and development economics work in NYC.

03

Urban Planning

Explore how cities are designed — transit networks, public space, housing policy. We look at what NYC gets right, what it doesn't, and what's next.

03 / What a Meeting Looks Like

More than just a lecture.

Each Wednesday we rotate between different formats to keep things engaging. Here's what to expect:

□

Case Studies

Deep dives into real NYC projects — we'll break down how Hudson Yards, the High Line, or a local condo flip actually happened, from permits to profit.

△

Design Challenges

Given a real site and constraints, sketch your own proposal. Past prompts: "Redesign Penn Station" and "Build affordable housing on a vacant Harlem lot."

◆

Market Watch

We track live NYC real estate data — sale prices, rental trends, new construction. Members present a deal or neighborhood they've been following.

&

Guest Speakers & Field Visits

Conversations with architects, developers, and urban planners working in the city. We also plan walking tours of significant NYC neighborhoods and buildings.

04 / Get Involved

Join ARC.

No prerequisites. No application. Just show up on Wednesday with your lunch and an interest in how the built world works. We're open to all Browning Upper School students.
